Why trust this

Real returns, not assumptions.

Most calculators assume the market politely returns 7% a year forever. We use 21 years of actual published Australian fund returns — including the year balanced funds lost 21.8%.

We checked our own work.

The real test is data the model has never seen. We trained it on 2004–2014 only, then predicted a cohort starting in 2015 — and the actual 2024 outcome landed inside the predicted range. Three further cohorts (A, B and C) were internal consistency checks that confirmed the simulator's mechanics; these used the same data window as the fitted parameters, so they test the plumbing, not the forecasting.

We tell you what we don't know.

No Age Pension yet, so our survival numbers are deliberately harsh. Twenty-one years of data is good, not infinite. The full methodology is one click away — read it and judge for yourself.

The most expensive thing you own might be a percentage.

A 45-year-old with $120,000 in super retires at 67 with about $600,000 in a fund charging 0.5% — in today's dollars. The same person in a 1.5% fund retires with about $517,000. The missing $84,000 went to fees. Yours is calculated above. Go look at it again.
